<p><p>The waterless coolant market primarily features two types: ethylene glycol and propylene glycol. Ethylene glycol is commonly used due to its efficient heat transfer properties and cost-effectiveness, making it suitable for high-performance applications. Propylene glycol, while slightly less efficient, is favored for its non-toxic nature, making it ideal for environmentally sensitive applications and in systems where human exposure is possible. Both types enhance engine protection, reduce corrosion, and improve efficiency by minimizing coolant evaporation and boiling point elevation.</p></p>

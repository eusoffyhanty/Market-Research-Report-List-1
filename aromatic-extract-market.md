<p><p>The aromatic extract market includes two primary production methods: the Direct Chlorination (DC) process and the Hydrochlorination (HC) process. The DC process involves the direct reaction of aromatic compounds with chlorine, leading to chlorinated derivatives that are useful in various applications. In contrast, the HC process employs hydrogen chloride, resulting in a different set of products. Both methods cater to industries such as pharmaceuticals, agrochemicals, and polymer manufacturing, each serving specific requirements in terms of product purity and application suitability.</p></p>
